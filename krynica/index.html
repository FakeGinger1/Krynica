Why Krynica-Zdrój?


If you're seeking for a vivid place to spend a serene weekend or few weeks during any time of the year, then Krynica-Zdrój is a more niche destination which will provide you with a beautiful glimpse into Polands rich history.

Krynica has been considered to be a medical sanctuary for centuries with many sanatoriums and spas located in the town. This can be partially attributed to its excellent air quality as a result of the high number of trees surrounding the area, the good ventilation in the valley as well as a lack of industrial areas nearby. Another aspect is the minerally packed spring water sources which can be found in the area. To read more about these incredible waters, visit our history page.


What Krynica-Zdrój Offers


This charming town offers many tourits attractions among them being:

  • Amazing and plentiful winter sport facilities
  • Hiking/biking trails as well as picturesque walking paths
  • Mineral water houses called pump rooms
  • A tree-top path and 49.5 metre tower
  • Three historic museums.

Krynicas' Geography


Krynyca Zdroj is a small town tucked away in Southern Poland. In the east of one of the two strips of the Beskidu Sądecki mountains, the town lies in the valley of four streams (the Kryniczanka, Palenica, Slotwinka and Czarny potok).

Krynicas' Climate


The microclimate of Krynica-Zdroj is defined by an extended summer period full of long sunny days as well as a lengthy period of snow cover during the winter months (usually from November to March). Krynica also typically isn't very rainy however it does experience an increase of rainfall in June due to the increase in pressure which is accompanied by thunderstorms.
