Recommended Spots!

Attractions In the City

Krynica-Zdrój has catered to visitors since the town's influx of tourists in the mid 1800s when it became a hotspot for spas and sanatoriums. One of the best places to begin exploring Krynica is the promenade ("deptak" in polish) which spans the length of the settlement. City promenades are vital aspects of every Polish town and each one tells a story of its home. Walking along the path you will be able to witness the gorgeous and colourful structures that make up the town. You'll meet many historical statues along the way, however never stray too far away from the nature aspect that is the heart of Krynica as the city is seamlessly intertwined with its surroundings.

On your walk along the promenade you can find many museums in the beautiful villas on your path. Krynica-Zdrój houses 5 museums:

  • The Nikifor Museum
  • The Toy Museum
  • The Hunting Museum
  • The Mountain Tourism Museum
  • The Regional Museum

All within walking distance of each other! The Kryniczanka river runs throughout the town and you'll be able to see its rushing current on your walk. Many extravagant fountains are also dotted throughout the town. In the summer, gorgeous flower arrangements and decorations are grown in the city. Famously, in the town square, a 2 metre tall peacock flower arrangement is grown beside flowers spelling out the town's name behind it, further blurring the line between nature and cityscape.

The Mountain

Krynica-Zdrój sits right at the base of Parkowa mountain (Góra Parkowa) which reaches 741 meters. You can reach the peak by riding the funicular train (built 1937) to quickly see the attractions. The ride is a wonderful as the slow climb allows you to see the surroundings you'd not see otherwise.

Funicular Train, See [22]


Activities

By ascending the mountain via a series of wooden sloped pathways you can see the following along your way up:

  • Nineteenth-century wooden gazebos
  • Charmingly built mineral water pump rooms
  • Many statues, among them the forest shrine of Our Lady Queen of Krynica-Zdrój.
  • Countless ponds including a swan pond surrounded by ancient trees.

During the winter the peak of the mountain is used for skiing, snowboarding and sledding, with beautiful immersive light displays at night. In the summer exhibitions are put up showing the history of the town and you can better enjoy the surrounding Zdrojowy park. After the walking you can rest up and get your energy back in one of the restaurants at the top!

Attractions In the Nature

Krynica will be a match made in heaven for a nature and sport lover at any time of year. In the summer you can enjoy countless different hiking, hillwalking and cycling paths along rivers, mountains and beaches. However, the winter sports facilities is where Krynica-Zdrój really shines. You can practically try a new ski resort every day as there is one situated in most of the mountains surrounding the town. Słotwiny Arena, one of the main ski resorts, itself offers 8.1 km of ski tracks and 10 different ski trails of multiple difficulties. The summit has an elevation of 863 metres so not only does it offer an incredible ski route but otherworldly views as well.

What is the Tree Top Tower?

In 2019 a lookout tower and tree top pathway was built near the top of one of the western mountains of Krynica. The project cost a whooping 15,700,955.87 PLN (or £2,943,379). The tower's wooden structure beautifully complements the surrounding forest and seamlessly blends in with the environment.

It is held up by 100 wooden poles and reaches up to 49.5 metres high which equals to 15 stories tall! Taking a walk on this 1,030 metre pathway among the tall and dense pine tree forest of Slotwiny brings an spectacular view of Krynica-Zdrój, Góra Parkowa, Jaworzyna Krynicka and Beskid Sądecki mountains. The path is also fully wheelchair acessible with the walkways being on an incline of 2-6%. The way up to the tower is beautiful in itself as it takes you through winding mountain roads which run through the thick forests around Krynicas mountains.


On the Path

If the incredible views weren't enough, the wooden path is decorated by information boards providing context and fun facts about the surrounding wildlife, nature and history. You can also finish this spectacular walk off with a fun and quick descent on a 60 metre slide, not just for kids! Your expression on the way down will be captured in a picture and can be purchased at the bottom of the tower so you can keep hold of the fun memory!

During the summer concerts are held below the tower, pairing music with the exceptional view. These concerts have a serene atmosphere when surrounded by dark forest and lit up by candles in the warm Polish summer. In the winter you can take the short walk to one of the neighbouring ski resort on Słotwina to make the most of the thick winter snowfall. You can see some of the great mountain views while enjoying some winter sports like skiing, snowboarding or even sledding on the smaller hills!
